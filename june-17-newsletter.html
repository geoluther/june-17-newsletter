Summer Happenings at KGNU

We at KGNU support you in upholding your unalienable right to the pursuit of happiness, whether you are attending one of the many KGNU Presents summer music events, or one of the KGNU Resistance Radio Calendar activities defending health care, worker’s rights, civil rights, women’s rights, GLBTQ rights, communication rights, or other long-fought-for freedoms that have come under increasing attack. In honor of our right to pursue happiness, KGNU dedicates the summer membership drive to the Summer of Fun, filled with the programming and activities you depend on, enjoy, and engage in all summer.

With your help, KGNU needs to raise $30,000 to sustain a summer of community programming that includes many live broadcasts from your favorite festivals and events. Your new or additional contributions and ongoing support make KGNU possible. Help us knock a day or two off our Summer of Fun Membership Drive this July 11th through July 16th by contributing today.

At KGNU, we’re planning on pursuing happiness throughout the summer. Whether you join us at the KGNU Boulder Studios on Bike to Work Day to enjoy fresh-baked cobbler and Ozo coffee at our breakfast station, try your luck at winning a few great summer festival ticket giveaways, help take Summer of Fun Membership Drive pledges, pledge your support with a few bucks; join KGNU for our exclusive live broadcasts of RockyGrass or the Arise Festival; or come with KGNU to the 27th Annual Rocky Mountain Folks Festival, NedFest, or KGNU's very own 29th Annual Charles Sawtelle Mountain Jam at the Gold Hill Inn, you have every right to enjoy your summer with KGNU. Let's do it together! Call (303) 449-4885 or pledge your support online today.

Thank you! Support like yours has kept KGNU amplifying community voices, music, and culture for 39 years!

Bike to Work Day - Have Breakfast @ KGNU June 28th!

Community Cycles has organized more than 60 local businesses in Boulder to host breakfast and bike service stations to serve food and drinks for commuters on June 28th for Bike to Work Day from 6:30 to 9 a.m. KGNU is one of the breakfast stations! Come win tickets to a concert or an annual membership and go on a tour to see how what you hear on air is made.

Ride (or walk) by our Boulder station at 4700 Walnut St. for breakfast June 28th, we’d love to see you!

KGNU's 29th Annual Charles Sawtelle Memorial Mountain Jam

With Laurie Lewis & The Right Hands, Masontown, and Thunder and Rain

"The Charles", a KGNU tradition, is a family-friendly event for music fans who enjoy clear mountain air, dazzling acoustic music, and great food in a spectacular setting. The festivities get started at 11:30am and run until 4:30pm on Sunday, July 23rd.

Tickets are $20 for KGNU members, $25 for non-members and are available online or by calling KGNU at 303-449-4885 weekdays between 9:00am and 5:00pm. Tickets $5 more day of show. Children 12 and under are free. Food and beverages are available for purchase. No pets or coolers.

More Summer Music Happenings

RockyGrass!

This July 28-30th marks our 10th year broadcasting live from RockyGrass, featuring Del McCoury, Sam Bush Bluegrass Band, Earls of Leicester, Infamous Stringdusters and many more.

In addition to our regular complete live stream of the festival via KGNU.org and AfterFM.com , we’ll be broadcasting more of the music from the festival over our airwaves than ever before, We hope you’ll join us at the festival or through our broadcast for this exciting Colorado music event, now in its 45th year.

Arise!

The following weekend, August 4-6, KGNU will also provide an AfterFM live stream of the Arise Music Festival. Check in with our streaming page for updates on our streaming schedule as the festival approaches.

These are only a few of the music-related events KGNU will be involved in this summer, so check out our complete list of KGNU concerts and outreach events in the coming months.

KGNU and the League of Women Voters of Boulder County

KGNU is partnering with the League of Women Voters of Boulder County on a vmonthly commentary series called Making Democracy Work for All! which focuses on educating listeners on the workings of state and local government and letting them know how they can get involved at different stages of the political process. This month Mary Anna Dunn, Health Care Team Leader for the League of Women Voters of Boulder County, shares some thoughts about mental wellness. Click here to read more.

Laura Flanders Returns to KGNU

The show has started broadcasting on Friday afternoons at 3.30pm on KGNU, in place of FSRN the weekly edition, which discontinued production in April.

Laura Flanders is no stranger to the KGNU airwaves as the former host of Counterspin and regular guest on other news shows from Democracy Now to Economic Update.

"The Laura Flanders show stars the people I think of as the brightest, forward thinkers from the worlds of arts, activism and entrepreneurship. People who have real reality based strategies and models for power shifting change.""

KGNU & Boulder Bookstore Radio Bookclub

KGNU is partnering with the Boulder Bookstore for our radio bookclub. Every month Arsen Kashkashian, head buyer at the Boulder Bookstore, selects a book to inspire the KGNU listening audience to read along together. We will then be joined live in studio by the author for a book discussion.

To celebrate Independent Bookstore day in April, we taped the bookclub at the Boulder Bookstore with author Peter Heller. This was Peter’s second visit, previously he joined us to talk about his novel The Painter. This time he discussed his new book Celine. Click here to listen to the interview.

July’s selection is Alexandra Fuller’s latest book: Quiet Until the Thaw. Tune in on Thursday July 27th at 9am to hear Fuller’s interview at the bookclub.
